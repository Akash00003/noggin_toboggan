Introduction

An estimated 600,000 ski and snowboarders sustain injuries while riding per year, and approximately 24 of them result in death.

To combat this scary statistic, safety helmets were put in place to protect riders. This special edition of Powder Magazine focuses on ASTM F2040-18: Specifications for Non-Motorized Snow Sport Helmets. This specification requires testing be done under the following conditions:

        -Drop Height: 1.2m

        -Mass: 4kg

        -Acceleration Maximum: 300 g

        -Velocity: 4.8 m/s

A team of engineers out of Storrs, CT has designed the Noggin Toboggan 5000 - the optimal helmet for safety and style. Following ASTM F2040-18 standards, they determined optimal parameters to be used during testing of an experimental design:

Potential Energy (PE) = mgh = (4kg)(10m/s)(1.2m) = 48J

Force (F) = ma =  (4kg)(300G)(10) = 12,000 N = 12 kN

Xmax = (2*48)/12 = 8 mm ; T = 1.25(Xmax) = 1.25(8) = 10mm

*Bottoming out occurs at 0.8T (thickness); assume helmet radius is 100 mm = 0.1 m)

Yield Stress (𝝈y) = F/(2𝝅rx) = 12,000 / (2𝝅*.1*0.08) = 0.24 MPa

This month’s edition will feature Noggin Toboggan 5000 material selection, manufacturing process, design functionality, helmet aesthetics, and helmet performance analyzed with ANSYS modeling software.  

HELMET DESIGN

  • Material Selection

Ski and snowboarding helmets are made up of a high-impact plastic shell and a shock absorbing padding inside. The Noggin Toboggan 5000 features an Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene (ABS) plastic outer layer padded by Polyimide Foam 1500 (PIF-1500). PIF-1500 is a closed-cell rigid foam with a tensile strength of 11 MPa. It has a high glass transition temperature due to cross-linking that stabilizes the molecular chains within the foam, and excellent tensile and flexural properties.  The density of PIF-1500 is 65 kg/m3 which is on the rigid end of the spectrum. The 1500 in the name refers to the calculated molecular weight of the foam. As seen in Figure 1 (left), PIF-1500 is a closed-cell foam with approximately equal-sized cells. While closed-cell foam is more expensive than open-cell foam, it is better suited for temperature regulation and shock absorption.

  • Manufacturing Process
    Rigid Polyimide Foams are fabricated via a thermal foaming method of nadimide-end-capped polyester-amine oligomer (NPEAO) precursor particles in a closed mold [2]. The density and morphology of the foams can be controlled by the foaming temperature.

    There are two methods of helmet construction: in-mold and injection mold. The Noggin Toboggan 5000 will be injection-molded as this method creates a helmet that is more durable. The helmet is manufactured using the PIF-1500 foam bonded to the MIPS, and bonded to the ABS outer shell. The inner-padding foam and hard outer shell are seen in the figure to the left.  

    As for size, fit, and comfort, the helmet’s chin strap will feature a magnetic buckle - no more taking off your gloves in sub-zero temperatures to unclip! There will also be a dial on the back of the helmet to further adjust to the rider’s head.

TOTAL DESIGN FUNCTIONALITY

The total weight of the Noggin Toboggan 5000 is under 1lb. This is comparable if not lighter than  some of the top helmets in the market. This is important because in skiing and snowboarding the user will  be reaching speeds of up to and exceeding 40mph. At these speeds anything above this weight will be noticeably heavy on the user's head and after a day of snowsports will hurt their neck and be uncomfortable. The fit of the helmet is fairly tight, though comfortable. This is because any movement in the helmet will cause greater damage to the user in a crash. The tight fit is also important when putting goggles on because  the tighter the fit of the helmet, the better seal your goggles and helmet can make, allowing minimal air to flow onto your face and under your helmet.
                The shape of the Noggin Toboggan 5000 is designed to mimic the shape of a human head and goes completely around the backside of the head. On the front, the helmet is open, allowing the user to wear goggles and still have access to their mouth to eat, drink, or talk to their friends.
        With safety as the number one concern, the Noggin Toboggan 5000 is equipped with MIPS (Multi-Directional Impact Protection System). MIPS uses a slip plane design that mimics the brain's protective structure to manage energy from rotational impact. The MIPS technology is placed between the comfort padding and the crushable expanded polystyrene that lines the helmet, allowing the helmet to slightly slide. This technology is very important as it is very rare for a crash to occur in a linear fashion. Rather, most falls occur at an angle, which is what MIPS prepare for.    
                The Noggin Toboggan 5000 is smooth with eleven adjustable air vents. All vents can be adjusted to be open,  closed, or anywhere in between! There are three inlet vents on each side of the helmet that will allow air to flow in and an outlet on the rear of the helmet that all the inlets run through. This will allow air to flow into both sides of the helmet and out the back, bringing cold air through the helmet and allowing the user to control the temperature of their head by flowing heat out. There are also four vents on the top that will allow the user to release warm air out of the helmet, but not let cool air flow through. This allows them to let a little heat out, but not have as rapid of cooling as it would with the side vents. This design allows the helmet to have the least amount of air resistance and therefore allow the user to ski or snowboard faster overall and they will not have their head being pulled back by air, while also having the ability for the user to control their temperature in any conditions.

HELMET AESTHETICS

While protecting the head from injury is always the main priority, the desire to add style back into helmets is gaining traction. Like most skiing and snowboarding helmets, our design will include a goggle clip on the back to prevent a rider’s goggles from falling off for optimal vision. Several helmet designs on the market include an attachment for a GoPro camera - but they are too close to the brim of the helmet and prevent the rider from lifting their goggles - our design will feature a GoPro mount slightly farther back from the rim so riders can still catch sweet highlight videos without compromising a place to put their goggles. 

        

Staying warm is a vital part of winter sports. Most helmets already have coverings for your ears for warmth, but our ear coverings do more than that. Our helmet includes a bluetooth connection to your phone to blast music, talk to your friends further down the slope, or order a waffle to be waiting for you at the lodge.

“If you’re not falling, you’re not going hard enough.” 

Crashes are inevitable, so our helmet, equipped with MIPS, will also feature an attachment that measures the forces a rider absorbed during a fall. It will use the data output to create contour charts of the head and measure the deformation of the helmet’s interior padding. This will inform the rider if they sustained forces beyond the threshold of a concussion. If the rider does not respond by clearing an alert on the helmet within 3 minutes, emergency services are automatically notified as well as an emergency contact. This will give riders the confidence to catch big air even if they’re riding solo.

Polyurethane Foam and Polystyrene Foam. It is clear to see that

the Polyurethane remains intact while the Polystyrene almost completely flattens out. This is further reinforced by looking at the deformation values, where Polyurethane has a max deformation of 0.054m, while Polystyrene has a max of 0.1m, which is considerably higher. By looking at these values, it is clear to see that Polystyrene is able to retain almost no shape for a collision and is a poor material to choose for a helmet that would need to stay firm and protect the user’s head.

THICKNESS VARIATION RESULTS

Analysis: (Minimum 3 “Explanations”

        

In the top two images, a comparison can be made between two different Polyurethane models, the left with 0.75” thickness and the right with 2” thickness. While a clear difference cannot be seen solely looking at the animations, there is a difference present in numeric analysis. The 0.75” thickness results in a max deformation of 0.52m while the 2” thickness results in a max deformation of 0.048m. This shows that increasing the thickness helps strengthen the model and that we would need to increase the thickness until there is a minimal deformation that would not impact the user’s head.
